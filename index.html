Culinary Arts:
Apprentice Cook Program

Culinary Arts Skill Centre

Harry Ainlay Culinary Arts Apprenticeship Program can be attended for half a day from any high school in the Edmonton Public School Board!

Centred around hands on sensory learning in groups, focussed on a multi-activity cooking lab of demonstrations and practice in an industrial kitchen. The cooking focuses on modernist cooking techniques that are fundamental to properly developing flavour and textures. The curriculum set out by Alberta Education, in association with Alberta Apprenticeship and Industry Training, allows students to complete the first period apprenticeship cook exam after the two semesters of technical training.

Program Overview

The first year apprenticeship status* is granted after two semesters in the Culinary Arts Program and successful completion of the first period apprenticeship exam. A reference for preffered admission to NAIT's Culinary Arts, Retail Meat Cutting, and Baking programs can be attained by maintaining honors in every module.

The first semester begins with introductory and intermediate level courses, while the second semester is designed as an advanced level and build upon previous skill development. All students in the program receive certifications in Alberta Safety and Sanitation in the first semester.

Total Weighting Semester I

  1. Peer Evaluation & Instructor Evaluation 80%
  2. Sanitation Exam & Evaluation 10%
  3. Final Menu Item Practical Exam 10%

Total Weighting Semester II

  1. Peer Evaluation & Instructor Evaluation 90%
  2. Career Directions & Expansions 10%
  3. Opportunities and Apprenticeship Preparation 0%

*All marks are based on the completion and assessment on curriculum outcomes.

Semester 1

Every student in the class will take the Alberta Government Sanitation Examination after one week of training and will need at least a 70% to pass. This is followed by knife skills and a rotaion through a 5 station brigade system.

  • CKA3900: Apprenticeship Safety
  • CKA3401: Kitchen Orientation
  • CKA3406: Kitchen Mathematics
  • CKA3421: Cooking Proteins 1
  • CKA3441: Fruits & Vegetables
  • CKA3446: Starches & Salads
  • CKA3451: Breakfast & Dairy
  • CKA3456: Introduction to Baking
  • CKA3461: Breads & Cookies
  • CKA3466: Pies & Fillings

Semester 2

The first year apprenticeship exam can be challenged on completion of the semesters two CKA modules. Students also have the opportunity to join the Harry Ainlay Culinary Arts Team and compete in the High School Culinary Arts Competition and Skills Canada Competitions.

  • CKA3436: Thickeners & Sauces
  • CKA3431: Stocks & Soups
  • CKA3411: Meat & Seafood 1
  • CKA3416: Meat & Seafood 2
  • CKA3426: Cooking Proteins 2
  • CTR2310: Career Directions-Expansion
  • CKA3560: Practicum A
  • CKA3565: Practicum B
  • CKA3570: Practicum C
  • CKA3575: Practicum D

Cook Apprenticeship & the Registered Apprenticeship Program

It is recommended (if possible) for student who would like to continue on to a journeyman cook certification to complete their R.A.P. hours simultaneously with the Culinary Arts Program.
